The ABCDE Melanoma Rule

Early detection depends on identifying these crucial patterns in your skin lesions.

  • A - Asymmetry: One half of the mole does not match the other half.
  • B - Border: Edges are irregular, ragged, notched, or blurred.
  • C - Color: The color is not uniform; contains shades of brown, black, or pink.
  • D - Diameter: The spot is larger than 6 millimeters across (pencil eraser).
  • E - Evolving: The mole is changing size, shape, color, or symptoms like itching.

Understanding Melanoma & Skin Lesions

Skin cancer is one of the most common cancers worldwide. Melanoma is particularly dangerous due to its ability to metastasize rapidly. Regular examinations and screening help locate suspicious lesions early.

Important Medical Disclaimer: DermAI is an investigational screening assistant, not a primary medical diagnostics device. Always consult certified dermatologists for skin health concerns.

Lesion Prevention & Daily Care

Simple daily routines to secure your skin from UV radiation and environmental factors.

Sun Protection

Apply broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher every single day, even in cloudy seasons.

Wear Coverups

Use UV-blocking sunglasses, broad-brimmed hats, and light long sleeves during high UV index hours.

Peak Hour Limit

Limit outdoor activities between 10:00 AM and 4:00 PM when solar radiation is strongest.

Monthly Self Check

Observe all moles and birthmarks once a month and record changes to show your doctor.
